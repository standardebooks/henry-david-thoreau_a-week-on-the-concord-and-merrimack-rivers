(1) There is a section in chapter 3, beginning "being an ancient and capital seat" and ending "so they took leave and went away," that is rendered as a `<blockquote>` in the original but doesn't really work that way (being a mix of quotations and Thoreau's interjections); I have just rendered this as regular paragraphs. Another section in chapter 6 (from "The Hon. Wyseman Clagett" to "Squire MacGaw's") has a similar problem and I have tried to solve it by treating the `<blockquote>` formatting in the original as a mistake.

(2) I've modernized the spelling of many terms for e.g. American Indian peoples, Hindu texts and terminology.

(3) Some of the `<blockquote>` elements in the original were in quotation marks, others not; this seems to have been meant to distinguish Thoreau's original poems from those of other authors; I've omitted the quotation marks. In one case I split a `<blockquote>` into two `<blockquote>` elements when Thoreau was tacking a line onto someone else's poem ("Than fifty years of Europe...").

(4) There's a section in chapter 6 during Thoreau's digression on Friendship in which he gives examples of how friends address one another. I've converted this into an unordered list, which I think matches the intention better, though not the typography of the original.
